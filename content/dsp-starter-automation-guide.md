<p>Dyson Sphere Program does not hold your hand. Your first hour can feel overwhelming. Dozens of buildings, multiple resources, and no clear starting point.</p>
<p>Here is the exact 5-step path to get your first automated factory running.</p>

{{< diagram "starter-factory-pipeline.svg" "Starter factory 5-step pipeline" "720" >}}

## Your First Factory: 5-Step Setup

<h3>Step 1: Place Your First Miner</h3>
<p>Find iron ore on your starting planet. Open the build menu, select the Mining Machine. Place it directly on an iron ore patch. Connect it to power with a Wind Turbine.</p>
<p>A single mining machine covers 4-6 ore tiles and produces roughly 1 iron ore per second.</p>

<h3>Step 2: Feed a Smelter</h3>
<p>Place a Smelting Furnace near the miner. Run a conveyor belt from the miner output to the smelter input. The furnace turns 1 iron ore into 1 iron ingot per second. Add a Wind Turbine to power it.</p>

<h3>Step 3: Make Your First Component</h3>
<p>Place an Assembling Machine Mk.I next to your smelter. Belt iron ingots into it. Open the assembler and select Iron Ingot - Gear, or Circuit Board if copper is nearby.</p>
<p>One assembler makes roughly 1 gear per second. This is your first automated component.</p>

<h3>Step 4: Build Storage</h3>
<p>Place a Storage Box Mk.I at the end of your assembly line. Belt the finished gears into it. Now you have passive income of gears while you explore the tech tree.</p>

<h3>Step 5: Repeat for Essential Components</h3>
<p>Your first automation loop should produce at least three things in this order:</p>
<ul>
<li>Magnetic Coils (copper + iron) - for belts and sorters</li>
<li>Circuit Boards (copper + iron) - for almost everything</li>
<li>Gears (iron) - for belts, sorters, and assemblers</li>
</ul>
<p>Build each as a mini-line: miner, furnace, assembler, storage box. Three parallel lines, three full boxes after 30 minutes.</p>

## Why Most New Players Stall (And How to Avoid It)

<p>The biggest mistake beginners make: trying to build too much at once. A single mining machine feeding a single smelter feeding a single assembler is already automation. You do not need 20 buildings in the first hour.</p>

<p><strong>The 3-building rule:</strong> Before placing anything, ask what feeds this. If the answer is I don't know, you are building too fast. Each line should be: Raw Material, Processor, Output. Nothing more.</p>

## Scaling Your First Factory

<p>Once gears, coils, and circuits come in automatically, scale up:</p>
<ul>
<li>4 miners on iron (smelt into 8 furnaces, 4 belts of iron ingots)</li>
<li>2 miners on copper (smelt into 4 furnaces, 2 belts of copper ingots)</li>
<li>1 miner on stone (smelt into bricks for foundations)</li>
<li>1 miner on coal (thermal power and future needs)</li>
</ul>
<p>Get iron and copper stable first. Add belts between furnace banks and a central bus. Components from the bus feed into assemblers.</p>
