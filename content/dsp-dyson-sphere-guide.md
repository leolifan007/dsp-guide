<p>Building a Dyson Sphere is the endgame milestone in DSP. But the first attempt often fails: you launch sails, they expire, and you get zero power.</p>
<p>Here is the exact sequence to build a permanent, self-sustaining first layer.</p>

## TL;DR: The 4-Step Sequence

<p><strong>Build rockets first, not sails.</strong> A common trap: players launch thousands of solar sails and watch them expire (they last 1800 seconds - 30 minutes). Permanent sphere requires frame nodes, which require rockets. Build a rocket factory BEFORE scaling your sail launcher.</p>

<h2>Step 1: The Minimum Rocket Factory</h2>
<p>Each rocket costs: 2x Quantum Chips + 2x Graviton Lenses + 2x Rocket Payload + 4x Deuterium Fuel Rods. That is a heavy production line. Build a minimum of 1 rocket per minute to start - this feeds about 6-8 railguns.</p>
<p>Your rocket factory needs at least:</p>
<ul>
<li>2 quantum chip assemblers (fed by processors, coincident circuits, and titanium)</li>
<li>2 graviton lens assemblers (fed by processors and strange matter)</li>
<li>4 deuterium fuel rod assemblers (fed by deuterium and titanium alloy)</li>
<li>2 particle container assemblers for rocket payload (graphene + titanium)</li>
</ul>
<p>This is around 30 buildings. Build it on your home planet or a mining world with titanium and silicon.</p>

<h2>Step 2: Choose Your First Sphere Orbit</h2>
<p>Open the Dyson Sphere panel (press Y). Two orbit types matter for your first build:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Default orbit:</strong> 0 degree tilt, circular. Easiest to build. Good starting choice.</li>
<li><strong>Custom orbit:</strong> Adjustable tilt and radius. Higher radius captures more power but costs more rockets.</li>
</ul>
<p>For your first sphere, pick the default orbit at radius 25,000 km - 35,000 km. This balances material cost with power output.</p>

<h2>Step 3: Frame Construction Strategy</h2>
<p>The frame is the skeleton of your sphere. You build it with rockets. Each section of frame costs rockets and produces the nodes that capture sails permanently.</p>
<p><strong>Build the latitude ring first.</strong> Place a ring around the equator at 0 degrees. This is the cheapest permanent structure - only about 200 rockets for a full ring. Once the ring is up, every sail launched into the ring orbit becomes permanent.</p>
<p>Then add longitude lines connecting from the equator ring to each pole. Think of it as a grid: equator ring + 6-8 meridian lines. This grid structure costs roughly 1000-1500 rockets total.</p>

<h2>Step 4: Sail Injection (After Frame Exists)</h2>
<p>Never launch sails without an active frame node in their orbit path. Sails only become permanent when they intersect with a frame node. Without a node, they expire after 1800 seconds.</p>
<p>Build approximately 10-20 railguns for a starter sphere. Each gun fires about 1 sail per 2 seconds. Aim for the frame node orbit. Once you see sails attaching to the grid, you are generating permanent power.</p>

<h3>Ray Receiver Setup</h3>
<p>Place Ray Receivers on the planet surface and point them (press R) toward the sphere. Each receiver captures roughly 5-15 MW depending on sphere completion. You need about 20-30 receivers to notice a meaningful power boost.</p>
<p>Use Graviton Lenses in your receivers - they double output at the cost of one lens per 10 minutes of receiver operation. Send lenses via logistics.</p>

<h2>Common First Sphere Mistakes</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Rocket shortage:</strong> Build buffer chests. Rocket production is slow. A buffer of 200-500 rockets lets you build frame sections without waiting for production.</li>
<li><strong>Sails before frame:</strong> Every sail launched without a frame node is wasted. Only launch sails after the first frame node is in place.</li>
<li><strong>Too small an orbit:</strong> A small orbit (under 20,000 km) means fewer frame nodes, meaning fewer sail attachment points, meaning wasted sails.</li>
<li><strong>No lens production:</strong> Graviton Lenses double receiver output. If you are making processors, make lenses too.</li>
</ul>
